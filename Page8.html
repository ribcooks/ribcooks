Final Project Proposal

A Marble Run/Rube Goldberg Machine

Showcase the story of R2G: https://restaurant2garden.com/

Using a marble run

Project Concept

The project consists of three marble runs that demonstrate different methods of food waste management:

  • A hyperlocal, circular food waste management system
  • Municipal composting
  • No composting (landfill)

Alternatively, a single marble run could split into three paths, allowing people to choose between the R2G path, the municipal composting path, and the landfill path. The marble runs will simulate the journey of food waste through different stages, highlighting the benefits and drawbacks of each method.

The laser-cut marble run will have the following features:

  • Marble represents food
  • Hyperlocal composting: A closed circle where the marble never leaves
  • Municipal composting: The marble ends at a different location, requiring a restart
  • Landfill: The marble disappears or triggers an effect representing methane release

The marble runs will pass through various stations, such as the restaurant, collection bin, compost site, garden, and landfill.

Contingency Plan

To ensure the timely completion of the Rube Goldberg machine project by May 27, a contingency plan will be implemented. By May 20, critical steps will be identified, and spare dominos, marbles, and key components will be gathered by May 21. Problematic sections will be simplified by May 22, with regular tests conducted to catch and correct issues by May 24. Extra time will be allocated for troubleshooting and adjustments, especially in the final days leading up to the deadline. A detailed record of any problems and solutions will be maintained. If needed, help from others will be sought, and adjustments to the design or timeline will be made to ensure project completion by May 27.
